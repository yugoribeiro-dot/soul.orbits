# soul.orbits — Positioning

## One-line
**Evolutionary astrology for people who'd rather understand themselves than predict the future.**

## Positioning statement
soul.orbits reads the sky as a mirror, not a forecast. We use the daily transits — where the Moon is, what Mercury is doing, which planet is whispering — as prompts for inner work: self-awareness, pattern recognition, and conscious evolution. We are for the reader who has outgrown surface horoscopes but isn't ready to memorize ephemerides. Mystical in mood, plain in language. We translate the cosmos into questions you can actually sit with.

## What soul.orbits IS
- A daily mirror — transits framed as reflective prompts, not predictions
- Educational without being academic — natal chart literacy in plain English
- Aesthetically restrained — night-cosmos minimalism, never cluttered
- Spiritual but grounded — no toxic positivity, no guaranteed outcomes
- A pause in the scroll — slower rhythm than the algorithm rewards, on purpose

## What soul.orbits IS NOT
- Daily horoscopes by Sun sign ("Gemini: today is great for communication")
- Prediction-as-product ("you will meet your soulmate in November")
- Crystal-and-rituals dropshipping
- Astrologer-flexing — no jargon walls, no "as a Capricorn rising stellium in the 8th…"
- Wellness-girl aesthetic — no pastels, no script fonts, no "manifest your dream life ✨"
- A guru voice — we don't tell, we point

## The reader
- 25–40, mostly women, some non-binary, some men
- Has read astrology content for years; is bored of it
- Reads Susan Miller out of habit but rolls eyes at TikTok astrologers
- Drawn to Jung, IFS, somatic work, Buddhism — astrology is one lens among several
- Wants depth without dogma
- Will save a post they want to come back to; will unfollow anything that feels performative

## Voice north star
> *If a thoughtful friend who studied astrology for ten years texted you a one-paragraph reflection on today's sky — what would that sound like?*

That's the voice. Not the oracle. Not the influencer. The friend who's done the reading.
